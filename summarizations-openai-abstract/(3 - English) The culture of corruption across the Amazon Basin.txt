This text analyzes the pervasive culture of corruption in the Amazon Basin, highlighting its various forms from non-elite to elite corruption. Corruption infiltrates public and private sectors, spanning different jurisdictions. The text emphasizes the impact of corrupt practices on sustainable development, social contract erosion, and economic distortions. Venality in the region involves kickbacks, state capture, and disguise in the allocation of funds for infrastructure projects. The Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI) reveals the extent of corruption in the Amazon countries. Various manifestations of corruption, patronage systems, and societal connections exacerbate dishonest behavior, contributing to societal distrust and cynicism. The piece underscores the challenges in combating corruption, the complexities of unethical transactions, and the need for a comprehensive understanding of its impacts for devising effective solutions.
