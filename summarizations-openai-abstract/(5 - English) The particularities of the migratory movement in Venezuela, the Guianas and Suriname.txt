The text delves into the migratory movements in Colombia, Venezuela, the Guianas, and Suriname. It highlights that migration in the Colombian Amazon was influenced by armed conflict with FARC and drug trafficking. Despite the 2017 peace process, new criminal groups emerged post-guerrilla eradication. In the Guiana Shield, wildcat gold miners exploit resources across Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana. The Venezuelan government's exploitation of natural resources led to economic strategies that degraded national competitiveness. Guyana and Suriname heavily rely on mineral resources for economic sustenance. The Guiana Shield's heritage has largely evaded deforestation but has attracted miners seeking fortunes from Venezuela and Brazil. The text ultimately raises concerns over the region's environmental impact and geopolitical disputes, especially regarding contested borders and resource exploitation.
