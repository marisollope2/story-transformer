The internal migration history of Colombia was greatly affected by the civil war and the emergence of drug production due to conflicts with FARC. The illicit drugs trade led displaced peasants to cultivate coca under FARC's protection. After the Peace Process in 2017, criminal groups replaced FARC, triggering a land rush fueled by drug trafficking and cattle farming.

The Guiana Shield region, encompassing Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana, has attracted wildcat gold miners who exploit mineral resources. Venezuela's complex governance has led to a surge in its population in mining areas controlled by the military and businesses. In Guyana and Suriname, the exploitation of mineral resources remains a key economic driver, with plans for an international transport corridor in Guyana potentially changing land use dynamics.

The Guiana Shield has largely avoided large-scale deforestation seen in neighboring regions but has not escaped migration impacts. Tens of thousands of miners, both locals and immigrants, mine for gold in the region. Migration patterns are influenced by economic factors such as fluctuating gold prices, while the region's governance and resource management play a significant role in shaping migration trends.
