The text discusses the history of collective action, civil disobedience, and blockades in the Amazon basin. Indigneous resistance to oppression has fueled protests against inequality and poor working conditions. Tactics like highway blockades in Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador have been used to challenge inequitable land distribution and exploitative labor conditions. In Brazil, landless peasants have resorted to occupying land from absentee landowners due to unequal land distribution. The text also covers the impact of these protests on politics, such as successful non-violent tactics inspired by Gandhi and King. Examples include the landless peasant movement in Brazil and the campesino movements in Bolivia and Peru. The struggles for land rights and economic justice in these regions have led to significant social and political changes, showcasing the power and impact of civil protests and blockades in addressing systemic inequalities.
