The text discusses migration in Colombia, particularly due to armed conflicts with FARC and drug trafficking, leading to internal displacement. The peace process since 2017 has not halted migration, but rather spurred the emergence of new criminal groups. In Venezuela and the Guianas, the region has seen gold mining by locals and immigrants, impacting the sparse hinterlands. Venezuela's chaotic governance has led to a surge in population in mining regions controlled by the military. Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana heavily rely on mineral resources and have seen potential conflicts over borders and resources. The Guiana Shield, though less impacted by deforestation, has its own challenges with wildcat gold miners exploiting minerals. The author of the text, Timothy Killeen, shares detailed analyses in his book, "A Perfect Storm in the Amazon," discussing the complex issues around migration, resource exploitation, and conflicts in these regions.
