This text discusses the pervasive culture of corruption across the Amazon Basin, highlighting key ideas such as the various forms of corruption affecting public and private sectors, the impact on sustainable development, and the prevalence of both non-elite and elite corruption in different countries. It explores how corruption distorts investment decisions, the challenges in documenting and addressing corruption, and the role of societal systems like patronage in perpetuating unethical behavior. The text also touches on the hidden costs of corruption in large infrastructure projects and the lack of accountability for wrongdoers.
