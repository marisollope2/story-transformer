The Lava Jato investigation exposed extreme corruption in Brazil and Peru. In Brazil, the scandal involved Petrobras, where bribes were paid for favorable contracts. This led to widespread arrests and convictions, impacting influential politicians and the economy. The scandal also unraveled in Peru, implicating Odebrecht in overcharging and bribery spanning decades. The corruption extended to various infrastructure projects, causing significant financial losses and legal actions against officials from numerous countries.

The scandal's repercussions are still felt, with job losses, decreased investments, and economic damage. The scandal contributed to political shifts, potentially influencing the election of Jair Bolsonaro in Brazil. The impact in both Brazil and Peru highlights the far-reaching effects of corruption, leading to legal proceedings, financial penalties, and societal distrust.
