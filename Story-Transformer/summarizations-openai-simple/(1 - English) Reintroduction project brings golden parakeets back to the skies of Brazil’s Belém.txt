Conservationists are working on a project to reintroduce the endangered golden parakeet back into the wild in Belém, Brazil. These birds are highly sought after in the illegal pet trade due to their striking yellow plumage. The project, which started in 2018, aims to release a total of 100 birds to establish a new wild population. The golden parakeets play a vital role in seed dispersal of various plant species native to the Amazon, making them crucial for the ecosystem. Despite challenges like habitat loss and wildlife trafficking, the project has seen success as the released birds have adapted well and even reproduced in the wild. Efforts include breeding programs, education, and protecting the birds from poaching. However, reintroducing previously captive birds poses challenges in their adaptation to the wild. The long-term goal is to ensure the golden parakeet population grows naturally and self-sustains in the wild.
