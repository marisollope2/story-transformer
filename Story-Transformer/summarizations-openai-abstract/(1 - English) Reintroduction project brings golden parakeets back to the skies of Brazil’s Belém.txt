The golden parakeet, facing extinction due to the illegal pet trade and habitat loss, is being reintroduced into the wild in Belém, Brazil, after being locally extinct for a century. 50 parakeets released since 2018 have adapted well, even reproducing. Significant for ecosystem services, they aid in seed dispersal of native Amazonian plants. The species remains vulnerable due to habitat loss and trafficking, despite successful reintroduction efforts. Educational activities aim to raise awareness and protect the golden parakeet population. Challenges persist in reintroducing previously captured individuals. The project plans to release 50 more parakeets over the next two years to establish self-sustaining populations. Monitoring efforts track the released birds' movements. The hope is that through continued efforts, the golden parakeet population will thrive and self-replenish naturally.
