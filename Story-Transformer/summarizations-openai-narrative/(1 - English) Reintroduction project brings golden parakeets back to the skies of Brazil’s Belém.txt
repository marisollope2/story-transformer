In Belém, Brazil, the golden parakeet faces extinction due to the illegal pet trade and habitat loss. Conservationists are reintroducing the bird into the wild after being absent for a century in the city. 50 parakeets have been released, with plans for 50 more in the next two years. The colorful parakeet is crucial for ecosystem services, aiding in seed dispersal for plants like the açaí berry. The reintroduction project has been successful, with released parakeets adapting well to the wild and even reproducing. The effort is supported by the Institute for Forest Development and Biodiversity and the non-profit Lymington Foundation. In the lush Utinga State Park, the parakeets are acclimated for at least five months before release, learning to recognize local foods and predators. Public education initiatives aim to raise awareness of the parakeet's importance and advocate for their protection. While challenges exist, the project shows promise in revitalizing the golden parakeet population in Belém and beyond.
